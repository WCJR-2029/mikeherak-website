Writing is how I find out what I actually think.

Not the first draft — that's just noise moving from head to screen. I mean the third or fourth pass, when the sentences start doing what you intended and you realize you believed something slightly different than you thought you did.

I've kept journals for years. Private ones, mostly. The kind where you can be wrong and sloppy and change your mind without anyone noticing. But there's something that only happens when writing is meant to be read. A different kind of discipline. The reader (hypothetical, real, doesn't matter) makes you accountable to coherence.

---

This site isn't a professional blog. It's not a newsletter. It's not content marketing.

It's a place to think out loud about the things I can't stop thinking about — operations, pattern recognition, how systems fail, consciousness, what it means to build something that outlasts you.

> Writing is thinking made visible. The discipline of the sentence is the discipline of the thought.

I don't know yet what this becomes. That's the point.

## What you'll find here

Posts about things I'm figuring out. Some will be observations from work. Some will be personal. Some will be half-baked and I'll update them later when I know more.

The only rule: I won't write something I don't actually believe.
